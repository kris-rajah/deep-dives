01 / The Opportunity

A Scarce Digital Asset With a Hard-Capped Supply of 21 Million

Bitcoin is a decentralised digital currency created in 2009 by the pseudonymous Satoshi Nakamoto. It operates on a peer-to-peer network secured by cryptographic proof-of-work, with a programmatic monetary policy that halves the block reward every ~210,000 blocks.

04 / Bull Case vs Bear Case

The Structural Case Has Never Been Stronger

The structural case for Bitcoin has never been stronger — institutional infrastructure, supply scarcity, macro liquidity. But the cyclical risks are real. The honest assessment: mixed evidence with a bullish tilt.

trending_up

Bull Case: Generational Entry

1. Macro Liquidity Tailwinds

The Fed's 2025–2026 liquidity expansion — rate cuts, $220B in reserve management purchases, uncapped repo operations. 84% correlation between Fed liquidity injections and BTC prices, with 56–60 day lag.

2. Institutional Adoption Is Structural

47% of institutional investors expect 3%+ portfolio crypto allocations. BlackRock recommends 1–2%. $103B+ in U.S. Bitcoin ETF assets. Strategy (formerly MicroStrategy) holds 673,783 BTC. This infrastructure didn't exist during prior bear markets.

3. Supply Scarcity Post-Halving

April 2024 halving reduced block rewards to 3.125 BTC. ~19.7M of 21M coins already mined. Supply shock against dramatically higher ETF demand.

trending_down

Bear Case: It Gets Worse

1. The Cycle Isn't Over

If the four-year cycle holds, bear phases can deepen to -70% to -80%. That means $37,800 at -70% or $25,200 at -80%. Bloomberg Intelligence's McGlone has predicted a potential return to $10,000.

2. Fed Policy Risk

Kevin Warsh as new Fed Chair introduces uncertainty. If Warsh proves more hawkish or inflation re-accelerates, all risk assets face severe headwinds. BTC-equity correlation has increased since ETF adoption.

3. ETF Redemption Spiral

ETFs create a new dynamic: cascading outflows force fund managers to sell underlying BTC, amplifying downward pressure in a way that didn't exist in prior cycles.

UK Capital Gains Tax for Crypto

The Rules

check_circle

No Disposal = No Tax

Unrealised gains are not taxable. A position growing from £5k to £10k incurs £0 tax until sold.

account_balance_wallet

Annual CGT Allowance: £3,000

First £3,000 of gains per person per tax year are tax-free.

percent

CGT Rates

18% (basic rate) or 24% (higher rate) on gains above the £3,000 allowance.

calculate

Section 104 Pooling

All Bitcoin holdings are pooled; cost basis is the weighted average of all purchases.

group

Spousal Transfer Is CGT-Free

Transfer crypto to a spouse before disposal to double the household tax-free threshold to £6,000.

warning

HMRC Crypto Reporting Mandatory (2025/26)

Exchanges must share customer data under OECD CARF rules from January 2026.

Tax-Efficient Strategies

Annual CGT Harvesting

Before April 5 each year, sell enough to realise gains within the £3,000 allowance — then repurchase. Note: the 30-day bed and breakfasting rule may apply.

Tax-Loss Harvesting

If holding crypto at a loss while having gains elsewhere in a GIA, crystallise the crypto loss to offset gains.

Staged Profit-Taking Example

Sitting on a £5,000 gain? Sell enough to realise £3,000 this tax year (tax-free), then wait until after April 6 to sell the rest using next year's £3,000 allowance. Total tax saved: up to £960.

Example: £5k Invested, Bitcoin Doubles to £10k

If you hold (no disposal) Tax: £0
If you sell all (£5k gain) Tax: £360–£480
If you sell half (£2.5k gain) Tax: £0 (within allowance)
Verdict

The Asymmetry Is Overwhelming

Bitcoin at ~$69,000 is half price from its all-time high, with the strongest institutional infrastructure in its history. The risk is real but bounded. The asymmetry favours buying here.

What's Different This Time

$103B+ in ETF assets, 47% of institutions targeting 3%+ allocation, BlackRock recommending 1–2%. The demand floor is structural and permanent. Prior dips had none of this.

On-Chain Signal

Accumulation addresses at all-time highs (372,000 BTC), long-term holder selling exhausted, MVRV in the accumulation zone for the first time since May 2022 — the same signal that preceded the 2023–2025 rally.

The Bounded Risk

5 consecutive weeks of ETF outflows ($3.5B+) and a new Fed Chair create near-term uncertainty. If the four-year cycle holds, further downside to $45–55k is possible. But 40–50% corrections have historically recovered in 9–14 months.

Assessment

Buy. This is the shallowest major correction in Bitcoin's history, backed by institutional infrastructure that didn't exist in any prior cycle.

This is deleveraging, not capitulation. Realised volatility (38) is half the 2022 bear market level (70+). The plumbing is intact. The base case points to $100–150k within 12–18 months; the bear case is survivable; the bull case is transformational.
