Cartagena:
Caribbean Crescendo

A 500-year-old walled city on the Caribbean coast, built by the Spanish to store looted gold, now home to Colombia’s best cocktail bars, crumbling pastel-coloured colonial mansions draped in bougainvillea, and a nightlife scene that spills out of courtyards and onto rooftops until the small hours.

01 / At a Glance

Old Town Is Your Base. Getseманí Is the Night.

You’re arriving from Medellín after two days of ATVs, paragliding, and 18-course tasting menus. Cartagena’s job is different; it’s heat, salt air, slow mornings, cold cocktails, and one properly big night. The temperature will hit 32°C with Caribbean humidity, which means the pace naturally drops. Embrace it.

Ciudad Amurallada (Old Town)

The walled city. UNESCO World Heritage Site. Cobblestoned, car-free in parts, colonial mansions with internal courtyards, fairy lights, and bougainvillea. This is where you eat, drink, and start every evening.

02 / Day 1 - Wed 8 Apr

Travel + Old Town Sunset

Half-day in Medellín, short flight, then your first taste of the Caribbean coast. The flight from MDE to CTG is just over an hour; one of the easiest travel days of the trip. You land mid-afternoon with enough time to check in, shower off the Medellín dust, and hit the Old Town for sunset.

Day 1 Timeline

flight
14:29 MDE → CTG Arrives 15:37

~1 hour flight. Rafael Núñez airport (CTG) is tiny and only 10 minutes from the Old Town; the easiest airport transfer of the trip.

hotel
~16:00 Check In

Unpack, acclimatise. The humidity will hit you immediately; this is sea-level Caribbean, not Medellín’s spring-like mountains. Drink water, change into shorts and linen, and accept that you will be slightly sweaty for three days.

restaurant
~16:30 Late Lunch

You haven’t eaten since breakfast in Medellín. Drop bags and eat immediately. Pick one:

La Cocina de Pepina Getseманí • ~£6–10pp

Traditional Caribbean home cooking. Fried fish, coconut rice, patacones, cazuela de mariscos. Big portions, cold beers, no-frills. The kind of meal your Colombian grandmother would make if you had one. TripAdvisor 4.5/5.

Calle de la Sierpe, Getseманí

Di Silvio Trattoria Old Town • ~£6–12pp

Reliable Italian in the Walled City. Pizzas, pastas, cold beers. Open all afternoon so no risk of arriving between services. Safe bet if everyone just wants to eat now. TripAdvisor 4.5/5.

Calle de la Mantilla #3-56, Centro Histórico

La Cevichería Old Town • ~£12–20pp

Famous ceviche spot. Featured on Bourdain’s show. Fresh seafood, open-air seating, buzzy. Arrive early or queue. If you eat here now, pick somewhere different for dinner. TripAdvisor 4.2/5.

Calle Stuart 7-14, Plaza San Diego

directions_walk
~17:00 Old Town Stroll

Walk the walled city. The Old Town is compact; you can loop the main streets in an hour. Start at Plaza Santo Domingo, wander through Plaza de los Coches (the old slave market, now ringed by sweet stalls), past the Cathedral, and along the city walls towards the Baluarte de Santo Domingo. The light in the late afternoon is extraordinary; golden hour on 500-year-old stone.

wb_twilight
~17:30 Sunset Drinks on the Walls

The old Café del Mar closed in 2024 but the sunset is exactly the same: Caribbean gold dropping into the sea from 500-year-old fortification walls. Arrive by 5:30pm for a good spot.

El Baluarte de la Gente Baluarte Santo Domingo • ~£4–10pp

Community-run bar on the exact spot where Café del Mar used to sit. Same iconic sunset, cheaper drinks. Run by young Cartagena culinary graduates. Caribbean bites from £1.40, cocktails £4–8. Open 4pm–11pm.

El Baluarte San Francisco Javier TripAdvisor 4.2/5 • ~£8–15pp

Cartagena’s other on-the-walls sunset bar, near Hotel Charleston Santa Teresa. Craft cocktails, live music, less crowded. Open 4pm–2am.

local_bar
~19:30 El Barón Cocktails

El Barón on Plaza San Pedro Claver. Mezcal old fashioneds, rum-based house specials, and cigar service if you’re feeling it. The terrace is the move. TripAdvisor 4.5/5. ~£6–9 per cocktail.

Cra. 4 No. 31-7, Plaza San Pedro Claver, Centro

restaurant
~20:30 Casual Dinner

Nothing fancy on night one. Save the big meals for Celele on Friday. Pick one:

La Cevichería Old Town • ~£12–20pp

Famous ceviche spot in the Walled City. Featured on Anthony Bourdain’s show. Fresh seafood, open-air seating, buzzy atmosphere. Arrive early or expect a queue. TripAdvisor 4.2/5 (4,600+ reviews).

Calle Stuart 7-14, Plaza San Diego

Sierpe Caribe Fusion Getseманí • ~£10–16pp

Caribbean seafood fusion in Getseманí. Fish from local fishermen, live music, great vibes. Black pasta with prawns, fish in brown butter. Travellers’ Choice winner. TripAdvisor 4.8/5.

Calle de la Sierpe #29-09, Getseманí

La Mulata Nr Castillo San Felipe • ~£6–12pp

Traditional Caribbean food at honest prices. Massive portions of fish, coconut rice, plantain, and the legendary Cazuela de Mariscos (seafood stew). Packed with locals. TripAdvisor 4.4/5 (4,000+ reviews).

Calle del Quero #9-58, near Castillo San Felipe

03 / Day 2 - Thu 9 Apr

Beach, Street Art & Alquímico

The recovery day. No tours, no schedules, no cultural obligations. Caribbean holiday mode: late wake-up, brunch, pool or beach, street art, maybe a massage, then one of the world’s best cocktail bars for the evening. The only thing you need to decide is where to lie down.

Day 2 Timeline

brunch_dining
Morning Slow Brunch

Zero alarms. Roll out whenever. Cartagena’s brunch scene is solid; sit in a colonial courtyard with coffee and eggs and pretend you live here.

Epoca Espresso Bar Getseманí • ~£4–8pp

Best specialty coffee in Cartagena. Small, unpretentious, excellent pour-over and espresso. Good pastries. The barista actually cares about extraction. TripAdvisor 4.5/5.

Mila Pasteleria Centro Histórico • ~£5–10pp

French-Colombian bakery. Croissants, eggs benedict, fresh juices. Pretty courtyard. TripAdvisor 4.5/5.

Café Luciérnaga Getseманí • ~£4–8pp

Relaxed courtyard café. Good avocado toast, smoothie bowls, strong coffee. Opens early by Cartagena standards. TripAdvisor 4.5/5.

pool
Afternoon Pool / Beach Club + Getseманí

Two options - beach/pool or streets. Or both. City beaches (Bocagrande) are fine but not spectacular; busy, hawkers, murky water. For a better experience:

Fenix Beach Club Tierra Bomba • 10 min boat • ~£12–20 entry

Beach club on a nearby island. White sand, loungers, bar service. Entry redeemable on food/drink. Boat runs every hour from Muelle de la Bodeguita. TripAdvisor 4.0/5.

Hotel Santa Clara Pool Old Town

Colonial monastery turned luxury hotel. Day passes sometimes available. Beautiful pool in a cloistered courtyard. Ask your concierge to call ahead.

Playa Blanca Barú • 45 min–1hr boat

The famous white-sand beach. Stunning water, very touristy. Better as a half-day trip. Can feel crowded.

Getseманí street art wander: Cartagena’s answer to Comuna 13 but looser and more DIY. Streets around Plaza de la Trinidad are covered in murals; political, abstract, beautiful. No guide needed; just walk and look. Key streets: Calle de la Sierpe, Callejón Angosto, Calle del Porvenir.

Optional massages: Several spots in the Old Town and Getseманí. Ask your accommodation for a rec. ~$25–40 for an hour.

restaurant
~19:00 Dinner before Alquímico

Eat first, drink after. All three are walkable to Alquímico. Pick one:

La Vitrola Old Town • ~£15–25pp

Classic Cuban-style restaurant in a colonial mansion. Live music, white tablecloths, excellent steaks and seafood. Has been the go-to Cartagena dinner spot for decades. Feels like Havana in the 1950s. TripAdvisor 4.5/5.

Calle del Santisimo #8-08, Centro Histórico

Candé Old Town • ~£10–18pp

Modern Colombian small plates in a buzzy courtyard. Good for sharing across the table. Creative cocktails, lively atmosphere, easy-going. TripAdvisor 4.5/5.

Calle del Colegio, Centro Histórico

Sierpe Caribe Fusion Getseманí • ~£10–16pp

Caribbean seafood fusion, live music, local fishermen’s catch daily. Travellers’ Choice winner. If you didn’t eat here on Day 1, tonight’s the night. TripAdvisor 4.8/5.

Calle de la Sierpe #29-09, Getseманí

local_bar
~21:00 Alquímico #11 World’s 50 Best Bars

Tonight’s centrepiece. The highest-ranked bar in Colombia and one of the best in Latin America. Three-storey colonial building in the Old Town: ground floor is an elegant cocktail lounge, first floor is a lively bar, rooftop terrace has open-air seating under the stars with views over the Old Town’s church domes.

Cocktails built around Colombian ingredients: aji peppers, tropical fruits, local honey, Colombian spirits. Menu changes seasonally. Tell the bartender what flavours you like and let them guide you. ~£7 per cocktail. TripAdvisor 4.5/5.

Calle del Colegio 34-24, Ciudad Vieja • Arrive 9pm for rooftop tables

04 / Day 3 - Fri 10 Apr

Rosario Islands + The Big Night

The best day of the Cartagena leg and arguably the best day of the trip. Island hopping in the morning, turquoise water and beach clubs in the afternoon, then the biggest night out of the Colombia stretch. This is the one you’ll remember.

Day 3 Timeline

coffee
~07:30 Quick Breakfast

Early start for the boat. Grab something fast before heading to the dock:

Epoca Espresso Bar Getseманí • ~£4–8pp

Quick coffee and pastry. Opens early. Best espresso in Cartagena. In and out in 20 minutes. TripAdvisor 4.5/5.

Mila Pasteleria Centro Histórico • ~£5–10pp

Croissants, coffee, fresh juice. Quick and reliable. TripAdvisor 4.5/5.

Street arepa de huevo <£1 each

Fried arepas stuffed with egg from any corner vendor. The fastest breakfast in Cartagena. Grab two and walk to the dock.

sailing
~08:30 Rosario Islands Boat Day

The Islas del Rosario are a national park of 27 islands about 45 minutes offshore by speedboat. Caribbean-postcard turquoise water, small beach clubs, and mangroves. Most tours depart from Muelle de la Bodeguita or Muelle Turístico at 8–9am.

speed Speedboat: Faster and more fun than the big slow catamarans. Book private or small-group if budget allows (~£40–65pp full day).
scuba_diving Islands: Isla Grande (beach clubs), Isla del Pirata (snorkelling), Cholon (party island, floating bars, music, controlled chaos).
backpack Bring: Sunscreen (reef-safe), cash for island food/drinks, waterproof phone case, towel.
beach_access
Afternoon Island Hopping & Switch-Off

The full switch-off. Swim, snorkel, drink rum out of coconuts, doze on a lounger. Most boat tours include lunch; fried fish, coconut rice, patacones. Simple, fresh, perfect.

Cholon Party island

Anchored boats playing music, vendors paddling up in canoes selling fruit and ceviche, people swimming between boats with beers. Gloriously chaotic. Spend an hour, take photos, move on.

Playa Libre / Isla del Encanto Quiet islands

Cleaner beaches, better for actual relaxation and snorkelling. The antidote to Cholon’s chaos.

Most boats return to Cartagena by 3–4pm. Head back, shower off the salt, nap, and prepare for the biggest night of the trip.

restaurant
~20:00 Blow-Out Dinner

Last full night before Bogotá. The plan: serious dinner, then Alquímico (all three floors), then rooftop bars, then wherever the night takes you.

Celele #48 World • #5 LATAM

The best restaurant in Cartagena. Full stop. Chef Jaime Rodriguez spent years resurrecting forgotten Caribbean ingredients from indigenous communities. 8-course tasting menu: Caribbean flower salads, confit pork with plantain, lobster tartare with coconut sorbet. Set in a low-key blue house in Getseманí. Door locks once service starts. Don’t be late. TripAdvisor 4.5/5.

~£70–100pp with pairing • +57 301 742 0389 • Book well ahead

Calle del Espiritu Santo, Cr 10c #29-200, Getseманí

Carmen Alternative

Established fine dining in a gorgeous colonial mansion. Five dining rooms including a courtyard built into a centuries-old fortress wall under massive trees. Contemporary Colombian with Caribbean influences. More “classic big night out” than Celele: polished, impressive, easier to book. TripAdvisor 4.5/5.

7-course ~£45–65pp • 11-course ~£92pp • reservas@carmencartagena.com

Calle 38 #8-19, Calle del Santisimo

La Vitrola Alternative

If Celele is booked or you want something less tasting-menu. Classic Cuban-style colonial mansion, live music, white tablecloths, excellent steaks and seafood. Feels like Havana in the 1950s. TripAdvisor 4.5/5.

~£15–25pp

Calle del Santisimo #8-08, Centro Histórico

local_bar
~22:00 Alquímico: All Three Floors #11 World’s 50 Best

You know the ground floor from last night. Tonight you do all three levels. Start downstairs, work your way up to the rooftop. Spend an hour or two here before moving on.

nightlife
Late Late Night Options
Casa Cruxada Getseманí

The late-night move. Part bar, part club: converted house with multiple rooms, a courtyard, and DJs on weekends. Energy builds through the night. Open late. TripAdvisor 4.5/5.

Mr. Babilla Getseманí • Salsa bar

Live music some nights. Sweaty, loud, brilliant. If you want to dance, this is the spot. On Calle del Arsenal, the street that doesn’t sleep. TripAdvisor 4.0/5.

Alquimia Terraza Old Town rooftop

Rooftop bar in the Old Town. Good for a change of scenery if Alquímico feels done. DJs, open air, views over the domes. TripAdvisor 4.0/5.
