Cartagena:
Caribbean Crescendo

A 500-year-old walled city on the Caribbean coast, built by the Spanish to store looted gold, now home to Colombia’s best cocktail bars, crumbling pastel-coloured colonial mansions draped in bougainvillea, and a nightlife scene that spills out of courtyards and onto rooftops until the small hours.

01 / At a Glance

Old Town Is Your Base. Getseманí Is the Night.

You’re arriving from Medellín after two days of ATVs, paragliding, and 18-course tasting menus. Cartagena’s job is different; it’s heat, salt air, slow mornings, cold cocktails, and one properly big night. The temperature will hit 32°C with Caribbean humidity, which means the pace naturally drops. Embrace it.

02 / Day 1 - Wed 8 Apr

Travel + Old Town Sunset

Half-day in Medellín, short flight, then your first taste of the Caribbean coast. The flight from MDE to CTG is just over an hour; one of the easiest travel days of the trip. You land mid-afternoon with enough time to check in, shower off the Medellín dust, and hit the Old Town for sunset.

Day 1 Timeline

flight
14:29 MDE → CTG Arrives 15:37

~1 hour flight. Rafael Núñez airport (CTG) is tiny and only 10 minutes from the Old Town; the easiest airport transfer of the trip.

hotel
~16:00 Check In

Unpack, acclimatise. The humidity will hit you immediately; this is sea-level Caribbean, not Medellín’s spring-like mountains. Drink water, change into shorts and linen, and accept that you will be slightly sweaty for three days.

directions_walk
~17:00 Old Town Stroll

Walk the walled city. The Old Town is compact; you can loop the main streets in an hour. Start at Plaza Santo Domingo, wander through Plaza de los Coches (the old slave market, now ringed by sweet stalls), past the Cathedral, and along the city walls towards the Baluarte de Santo Domingo. The light in the late afternoon is extraordinary; golden hour on 500-year-old stone.

Community-run bar on the exact spot where Café del Mar used to sit. Same iconic sunset, cheaper drinks. Run by young Cartagena culinary graduates. Caribbean bites from £1.40, cocktails £4–8. Open 4pm–11pm.

El Baluarte San Francisco Javier TripAdvisor 4.2/5 • ~£8–15pp

Cartagena’s other on-the-walls sunset bar, near Hotel Charleston Santa Teresa. Craft cocktails, live music, less crowded. Open 4pm–2am.

El Barón on Plaza San Pedro Claver. Mezcal old fashioneds, rum-based house specials, and cigar service if you’re feeling it. The terrace is the move. TripAdvisor 4.5/5. ~£6–9 per cocktail.

03 / Day 2 - Thu 9 Apr

Street Food, Fortress & Alquímico

The touristy day, done right. A guided street food tour through the Old Town and Getseманí for lunch, the biggest Spanish fortress in the Americas in golden-hour light, then one of the world’s best cocktail bars for the evening. You’ll be too full from the food tour for a proper dinner – which is exactly the excuse you need to eat light at Alquímico and focus on the cocktails.

Day 2 Timeline

coffee
Morning Light Coffee & Pastry

Keep it light. The food tour at midday will be enormous – every single review says “don’t eat breakfast.” A coffee and a pastry is all you need.

Epoca Espresso Bar Getseманí • ~£4–8pp

Best specialty coffee in Cartagena. Small, unpretentious, excellent pour-over and espresso. Good pastries. In and out in 20 minutes. TripAdvisor 4.5/5.

restaurant_menu
~12:00 Street Food Tour 4.9☆ • 1,168 reviews

A guided walking tour through the Old Town and Getseманí with 10 tastings of Colombian street food: ceviche, arepa de huevo, exotic fruits, cocadas, pandebono, craft beer, and Colombian coffee. This IS lunch – every reviewer says you’ll be too full for dinner afterwards. 2.5–3 hours, max 12 people, English-speaking guides.

Nexperience Street Food Tour ~£36pp • 2.5–3 hrs

Meet at Plaza Cervantes (Camellón de los Mártires) – look for the orange umbrella. Route goes through La Matuna, Portal de los Dulces, San Diego, Plaza Fernández Madrid, then into Getseманí. Ends at Calle de la Sierpe.

Repeatedly named guides: Alvaro, Robert, Liz, Mercedes, Fernando. All get glowing recent reviews (Feb 2026). Vegetarian/dietary needs accommodated if you notify in advance.

Book on Viator Book on GetYourGuide

Free cancellation up to 24 hours in advance on both platforms. Check availability for midday departure times on 9 April.

hotel
~15:00 Siesta

Back to the accommodation. Rest, shower, let the food settle. The hottest part of the day – stay inside. San Felipe is much better in the late afternoon.

fort
~16:30 Castillo de San Felipe de Barajas

The largest Spanish fortress ever built in the Americas. Constructed in the 1600s to defend against pirates and rival empires. Underground tunnels, massive ramparts, cannons, and panoramic views over the city and harbour. UNESCO World Heritage Site. Budget 1.5–2 hours to explore properly.

Castillo de San Felipe de Barajas ~£10pp • 1.5–2 hrs

Entry: COP 50,000 (~£10pp). Audio guide +COP 15,000 (~£3) – worth it for the tunnel history. Open daily 8am–6pm, last entry 5:30pm. TripAdvisor 4.5/5, ~18k reviews.

Wear sturdy shoes (uneven stone everywhere), bring water, and wear sun protection. Arriving at 4:30pm dodges the worst heat and gives you golden-hour light on the ramparts.

~10 minute taxi from the Old Town, or a 20-minute walk from the Clock Tower via Calle del Arsenal. Walk back through Getseманí afterwards to catch the street art murals on Calle de la Sierpe and Callejón Angosto.

Calle del Colegio, Centro Histórico

local_bar
~21:00 Alquímico #11 World’s 50 Best Bars

Tonight’s centrepiece. The highest-ranked bar in Colombia and one of the best in Latin America. Three-storey colonial building in the Old Town: ground floor is an elegant cocktail lounge, first floor is a lively bar, rooftop terrace has open-air seating under the stars with views over the Old Town’s church domes.

Cocktails built around Colombian ingredients: aji peppers, tropical fruits, local honey, Colombian spirits. Menu changes seasonally. Tell the bartender what flavours you like and let them guide you. ~£7 per cocktail. TripAdvisor 4.5/5.

04 / Day 3 - Fri 10 Apr

Rosario Islands + The Big Night

The best day of the Cartagena leg and arguably the best day of the trip. Island hopping in the morning, turquoise water and beach clubs in the afternoon, then the biggest night out of the Colombia stretch. This is the one you’ll remember.

Day 3 Timeline

coffee
~07:30 Quick Breakfast

Early start for the boat. Grab something fast before heading to the dock:

Epoca Espresso Bar Getseманí • ~£4–8pp

Quick coffee and pastry. Opens early. Best espresso in Cartagena. In and out in 20 minutes. TripAdvisor 4.5/5.

Mila Pasteleria Centro Histórico • ~£5–10pp

Croissants, coffee, fresh juice. Quick and reliable. TripAdvisor 4.5/5.

Street arepa de huevo <£1 each

Fried arepas stuffed with egg from any corner vendor. The fastest breakfast in Cartagena. Grab two and walk to the dock.

sailing
~08:30 Rosario Islands Boat Day

The Islas del Rosario are a national park of 27 islands about 45 minutes offshore by speedboat. Caribbean-postcard turquoise water, small beach clubs, and mangroves. Most tours depart from Muelle de la Bodeguita or Muelle Turístico at 8–9am.

speed Speedboat: Faster and more fun than the big slow catamarans. Book private or small-group if budget allows (~£40–65pp full day).
scuba_diving Islands: Isla Grande (beach clubs), Isla del Pirata (snorkelling), Cholon (party island, floating bars, music, controlled chaos).
backpack Bring: Sunscreen (reef-safe), cash for island food/drinks, waterproof phone case, towel.
Deep Dive: Boat Operators & Booking →
beach_access
Afternoon Island Hopping & Switch-Off

The full switch-off. Swim, snorkel, drink rum out of coconuts, doze on a lounger. Most boat tours include lunch; fried fish, coconut rice, patacones. Simple, fresh, perfect.

Cholon Party island

Anchored boats playing music, vendors paddling up in canoes selling fruit and ceviche, people swimming between boats with beers. Gloriously chaotic. Spend an hour, take photos, move on.

Playa Libre / Isla del Encanto Quiet islands

Cleaner beaches, better for actual relaxation and snorkelling. The antidote to Cholon’s chaos.

Most boats return to Cartagena by 3–4pm. Head back, shower off the salt, nap, and prepare for the biggest night of the trip.

restaurant
~20:00 Blow-Out Dinner

Last full night before Bogotá. The plan: serious dinner, then Alquímico (all three floors), then rooftop bars, then wherever the night takes you.

Celele #48 World • #5 LATAM

The best restaurant in Cartagena. Full stop. Chef Jaime Rodriguez spent years resurrecting forgotten Caribbean ingredients from indigenous communities. 8-course tasting menu: Caribbean flower salads, confit pork with plantain, lobster tartare with coconut sorbet. Set in a low-key blue house in Getseманí. Door locks once service starts. Don’t be late. TripAdvisor 4.5/5.

7-course ~£45–65pp • 11-course ~£92pp • reservas@carmencartagena.com

If Celele is booked or you want something less tasting-menu. Classic Cuban-style colonial mansion, live music, white tablecloths, excellent steaks and seafood. Feels like Havana in the 1950s. TripAdvisor 4.5/5.
