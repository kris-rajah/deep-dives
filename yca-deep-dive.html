01 / The Opportunity

A Marked-to-Market Uranium Warehouse

Yellow Cake is not a data-centre equity. It is a Jersey-domiciled investment company that buys and holds physical U₃O₈ under a long-term offtake agreement with Kazatomprom. The AI/data centre link is second-order: power demand → nuclear build-out → structurally higher uranium prices → higher NAV.

What You Are Buying

warehouse

Physical Uranium

21.7m lbs of U₃O₈ stored in Cameco/Orano facilities in Canada and France. Rising to >23m lbs after 2025 purchase option.

handshake

Kazatomprom Supply

Long-term framework agreement with the world's largest producer. Recent $175m equity raise to exercise the 2025 option at ~$75/lb.

groups

Minimal Overhead

2 employees. No operating business in the usual sense — revenue and earnings are dominated by fair-value movements in uranium inventory.

The Volatility Pattern: Routine 25–35% Intra-Year Drawdowns

YCA routinely experiences 25–35% drawdowns even in years with strong positive returns. The 2024 drawdown of -35.5% was the deepest since IPO, driven by uranium spot weakness and NAV discount widening. Yet the stock recovered +16% in 2025 and is up another +10% YTD in 2026. This volatility is the price of holding a concentrated commodity proxy — not a signal to act.

03 / Macro & Sector Thesis

AI Data Centres → Nuclear Build-Out → Uranium Deficit

The thematic link is second-order but real and increasingly recognised. Hyperscalers are signing nuclear PPAs and SMR deals to secure baseload for AI clusters. This tightens an already-constrained uranium market and supports structurally higher prices.

YCA Management View

“Significant new demand from hyperscalers and the rapidly growing sector of data centres” reinforces the uranium bull case and their decision to raise equity to buy more physical.

The Second-Order Trade

You are not getting direct exposure to data centre rents, SMR IP, or EPC upside. You are getting exposure to the commodity whose demand is being structurally underwritten, in part, by that growth. If the AI/data centre thesis is right, it tightens the already-constrained uranium market and supports higher prices, which flow cleanly into NAV.

04 / Risk Assessment

The Cleanest Uranium Exposure Comes With Concentrated Risk

YCA’s simplicity is both its strength and its vulnerability. The business model avoids mine risk but concentrates exposure on uranium spot price, a single supplier, and periodic dilution through equity issuance.

public

Macro & Commodity

Uranium Price Risk

If new supply (restarts, greenfield projects) comes online faster than expected or demand disappoints (SMR delays, nuclear pushback), spot could correct sharply. Historically, uranium shows long flat periods followed by violent spikes — timing risk is substantial.

Energy Transition & Policy

A serious nuclear incident, policy reversal, or green backlash could stall reactor build-out. Regulatory delays for SMRs could push the “AI power” thesis timing beyond 2032.

FX Exposure

Uranium inventory priced in USD; shares trade in GBP. Currency moves can amplify or dampen NAV translation.

business

Company-Specific

Kazakh Concentration

Heavy reliance on Kazatomprom and Kazakhstan. Any sanctions, export restrictions, or political disruption could affect availability or pricing. Mitigant: inventory already stored in Canada/France.

Dilution via Equity Issuance

Business model requires periodic equity issuance to expand holdings. Recent raise: >$175m. Above NAV = accretive; below NAV = dilutive. Execution discipline matters.

Discount/Premium Volatility

In euphoric phases, YCA can trade at a meaningful premium to NAV. In risk-off environments, discounts can widen sharply, temporarily decoupling share performance from spot.

Verdict

Suitable Satellite Position. Convex Upside to Nuclear/AI Power.

For a well-diversified portfolio already long AI, utilities, and infrastructure, a modest allocation to YCA complements more direct AI/data centre exposures by adding convex upside to a structural nuclear/uranium bull case, at the cost of higher cyclical and geopolitical risk.
