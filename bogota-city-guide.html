01 / At a Glance

Chapinero Is Your Base. El Chato Is the Point.

You’re stepping off the plane from 32°C Cartagena into 15°C mountain air at 2,640m. The altitude is real: shortness of breath, dry air, lower alcohol tolerance. Drink water, go easy, and don’t sprint up any hills. You’re here for one night, one world-class dinner, and a taste of the capital before flying home.

02 / Sat 11 Apr

Arrival + El Chato

Fly in from Cartagena, feel the temperature drop, settle into Chapinero, then eat at the #1 restaurant in Latin America. That’s the whole plan. Don’t overcomplicate it.

Day 1 Timeline

flight
12:38 CTG → BOG Arrives 14:12

Short flight. Pack a jacket in your carry-on. You’re going from Caribbean heat to mountain cool in 90 minutes. El Dorado airport (BOG) is modern and well-organised.

hotel
~15:00 Check In, Chapinero

Uber or taxi from BOG, 30–45 minutes depending on traffic. Check in, unpack, adjust to the altitude. If anyone feels lightheaded, that’s normal. 2,640m is higher than most European ski resorts. Coca tea (mate de coca) is a local remedy, otherwise just water and rest.

directions_walk
~16:00 Chapinero Wander + Coffee

Walk the Calle 65–70 corridor, the restaurant-dense strip where El Chato and LEO live. Get your bearings before dinner. Altitude might sap your energy, so don’t push it.

Café Cultor Cl. 70A #9-44 • ~£2–4pp

Farm-direct Colombian coffee from 7 regions. Pick your brew method (Chemex, V60, AeroPress). Cosy casa-style space with a garden. Some of the best and most affordable specialty coffee in Bogotá. TripAdvisor 4.4/5.

Azahar Coffee Multiple locations • ~£3–5pp

Farm-to-cup specialty chain sourcing from Nariño, Huila, and Quindío. Multiple brew methods, all-day breakfast menu. Single-origin Colombian, beautifully brewed. TripAdvisor 4.4/5.

Libertario Coffee Roasters Calle 70A #5-37 • ~£3–5pp

Single-origin roaster sourcing from La Palma y El Tucán farm. Excellent pour-over, espresso, and flat whites. Relaxed vibe, small food menu. TripAdvisor 4.3/5.

restaurant
~20:00 Dinner at El Chato #1 Latin America

The main event. Chef Álvaro Clavijo trained at Noma and Per Se, then came home and opened a restaurant named after Bogotá slang for “mate.” Relaxed, unpretentious, convivial. No white tablecloths, no hushed reverence. Just creative Colombian food served with warmth and generosity.

El Chato Calle 65 No.4-76 • ~£40–60pp / tasting ~£130pp

À la carte ~£40–60pp or tasting menu with pairing ~£130pp. Mix and match across the table. Reservation fee: 50,000 COP (~£10) per person. Smart casual. TripAdvisor 4.0/5.

Why it matters: This is the best meal of the trip. Colombian ingredients treated with world-class technique. Creative without being fussy, familiar without being boring. Order generously.

nightlife
~23:00 Post-Dinner Drinks

You’ve been travelling all day, you’re at altitude, and you fly out tomorrow night. A few drinks after El Chato and an early-ish night is the smart play.

Huerta Coctelería Artesanal Chapinero • ~£6–10pp

Hidden-garden cocktail bar using herbs, flowers, and fruit grown in their own garden. Over 700 plants for homemade ingredients. Feels like stepping into a secret courtyard. Walking distance from El Chato. TripAdvisor 4.5/5.

Bogotá Beer Company Cra 5 #71A-75 • ~£4–8pp

Colombia’s most awarded craft brewery. Eight artisan beers on tap including the Chapinero Porter. Good burgers, nachos, and pizzas. Open until 2am on weekends. TripAdvisor 4.0/5.

Cervecería Gigante Chapinero • ~£4–8pp

Three-storey craft beer pub with tasting room. American-style bar with deep house and techno. Natural ingredients, German and English brewing techniques. Lively weekend crowd. TripAdvisor 4.5/5.

03 / Sun 12 Apr

Brunch, Bogotá Highlights & Fly Out

No alarm. Sleep in. You’ve earned it after 10 days across Spain and Colombia. The flight to Madrid doesn’t leave until 21:10, so you have a full day. Just don’t overcommit. Long brunch, one proper activity, easy afternoon, airport.

Day 2 Timeline

brunch_dining
Morning Long Brunch

The last Colombian meal before you’re back on European time. Get there by 10–11am or expect a wait at the popular spots.

Salvo Patria Cra 4 Bis #58-60 • ~£10–20pp

One of Bogotá’s best brunch spots. Converted Chapinero townhouse, creative Colombian cuisine, locally sourced ingredients, great cocktails. Reservations essential on weekends. TripAdvisor 4.5/5.

Masa Calle 70 #4-83 • ~£3–6pp

Acclaimed artisanal bakery-café. Famous for sourdough, almond croissants, and legendary carrot cake. Good for a lighter start. TripAdvisor 4.3/5.

Café Cultor Cl. 70A #9-44 • ~£2–4pp

Farm-direct Colombian coffee from 7 regions. They grow, roast, and brew their own beans. Pick your method. If you want one last perfect Colombian coffee, this is it. TripAdvisor 4.4/5.

landscape
Late Morning Pick One Activity

Time for one proper Bogotá experience. Pick based on energy levels and weather.

Option A: Monserrate Cable Car ~28,000 COP • 1.5–2 hrs

Teleférico to 3,152m for panoramic views over the entire city and surrounding Andes. Five minutes up, a lifetime of photos. Sun hours: 6:30am–5:30pm. Take it slow at the top. You’re going from 2,640m to 3,152m.

Option B: Parque 93 + Zona G Stroll Flat & easy

If the weather is grey or energy is low, skip the mountain. Leafy park surrounded by restaurants and cafes. Zona G (Gourmet Zone) is Bogotá’s fine-dining strip. Usaquén Flea Market (Sundays only, ~20 min Uber north) is also excellent.

shopping_bag
Afternoon Final Shopping & Airport Prep

Last chance to buy Colombian coffee beans, emeralds (Colombia produces 70% of the world’s supply), leather goods, or souvenirs. Grab packaged single-origin beans from Juan Valdez or Azahar. Cheaper than airport prices.

flight_takeoff
~18:00 Airport Transfer & Fly Out

Eat before the airport. BOG food is mediocre. Grab an early dinner in Chapinero, then leave by 18:00–18:30 latest. Uber to El Dorado ~£5–7.

Andrés Carne de Res DC Zona T • ~£10–20pp

Legendary Colombian steakhouse that doubles as a party venue. Massive portions, colourful decor, live music, and high energy. Perfect last meal for 5 lads. Short Uber from Chapinero. TripAdvisor 4.2/5.

Home Burgers Cra 5 #70a-74 • ~£4–8pp

Best burgers in Bogotá. Fresh ingredients, quality meat, great fries. Casual, fast, and cheap. Walking distance from the Calle 70 strip. TripAdvisor 4.3/5.

Salvo Patria Cra 4 Bis #58-60 • ~£10–20pp

If you didn’t go for brunch, come here for an early dinner. Creative Colombian food, good cocktails, relaxed converted-townhouse setting. TripAdvisor 4.5/5.

BOG 21:10 → MAD Overnight • ~10 hrs

Arrives Madrid 14:35 Mon 13 Apr. Sleep as much as possible. Short buffer before final LHR flight at 17:45.
