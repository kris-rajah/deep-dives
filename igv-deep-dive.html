03 / Thesis & Risk

AI Software Monetisation vs Valuation Distress

30–40% of IGV holdings have demonstrable AI revenue. Another 30–40% have no AI story at all. The fund is trading near its 52-week low with $24 billion in hedge fund short bets against software.

trending_up

Bull: AI Is Monetising

Microsoft (9.49%)

150M monthly active Copilot users. 20M GitHub Copilot users. AI revenue run-rate ~$13B. Commercial backlog $392B.

Palantir (8.19%)

FY2025 revenue +56% YoY. Net income +250%. US commercial revenue grew 137% in Q4. Customer base expanded from 20 to 500+. FY2026 guidance: 61% growth.

ServiceNow (3.80%)

Clearest enterprise AI story. 1,700 customers on Now Assist. AI products targeting $1B ACV by 2026. 2026 subscription guidance: $15.5B minimum.

Salesforce (7.33%)

Agentforce still in adoption phase. Narrative, not yet revenue.

Bear: Valuation & Distress

Valuation Distribution

Aggregate 29x is misleading — MSFT and ORCL mask extreme outliers.

Rate Sensitivity Paradox

Hiking cycle (2022–23): IGV -7.3%. Cutting cycle (2024–now): IGV -3.7% while QQQ gained +30.7%. Rate cuts should help software — but AI infra cannibalised the flows.

Current Distress

Near 52-week low ($76.68). Fund flows −$2.03B (12m). Hedge funds made $24B shorting software in early 2026, bets still increasing.

Concentration Risk: Top 3 = 25.63%

If MSFT + PLTR + ORCL de-rate simultaneously:

20% de-rate → ~5.1% fund loss
30% de-rate → ~7.7% fund loss
40% de-rate → ~10.3% fund loss

PLTR alone at 8.19% with >200x P/E could contribute 3–4% drawdown from a single earnings miss. This is base case risk, not tail risk.

04 / Verdict

At a Low Point ≠ Undervalued

The data does not clearly support buying IGV. It supports a specific, time-bound contrarian bet — if you have the conviction to make it against the weight of the evidence.
