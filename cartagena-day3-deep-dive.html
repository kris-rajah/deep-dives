Islas del Rosario

A 27-island national park archipelago, 45 minutes offshore from Cartagena by speedboat. Turquoise Caribbean water, coral reefs, beach clubs, and one gloriously chaotic party island. Everything you need to plan the best day of the trip.

03 / The Islands

Which Islands to Visit

The archipelago has 27 islands but most day trips focus on four main areas. Here is what each one is actually like.

landscape

Isla Grande

Beach clubs • snorkeling • relaxation

The largest and most developed island. Home to most of the beach clubs (Bora Bora, Encantada Beach, IslaBela). About 1 hour from Cartagena. This is where you go for a proper beach day with facilities, loungers, and food service.

Vibe: Relaxed-to-party depending on the beach club. The default choice for most day trips. Good snorkeling nearby.

celebration

Cholon

Party island • boat parties • controlled chaos

A tiny beach off an island near Baru. Colombia's most famous boat party spot. Multiple boats anchor in a bay, music blasting, people swimming between boats with drinks, vendors selling everything from ceviche to hookah. Hip hop, reggaeton, Coco Locos in waist-deep water.

Vibe: Spring Break-adjacent but not full MTV. Spend 1–2 hours, soak it in, take photos, then move on. Excellent as a stop on a private charter, less ideal as the whole day. See dedicated Cholon section below.

sunny

Isla Baru (Playa Blanca)

White sand • over-touristed • iconic photo

The most famous beach: long white sand, palm trees, turquoise water. Looks incredible in photos. Reality: overrun with mass tourism, aggressive vendors selling everything constantly, and significant crowding.

Vibe: Beautiful beach, frustrating experience. Worth a 30-minute photo stop on a private boat, but not recommended as your main destination. Vendors can be overwhelming –constant "no, gracias" required.

water

Quiet Islands

Isla del Encanto • Isla del Pirata • Playa Libre

Smaller, less developed islands with cleaner beaches and better snorkeling. Isla del Encanto has an upscale beach club with a pool and excellent sandy beach. Isla del Pirata is good for snorkeling. These are the antidote to Cholon's chaos.

Vibe: Genuinely relaxing. Better water clarity for snorkeling. Fewer vendors. Best for actual unwinding between Cholon's chaos and the big night ahead.

05 / Snorkeling & Marine Life

Is the Snorkeling Worth It?

scuba_diving Snorkeling

Quality: Decent but not world-class. Over 1,300 species of marine animals and plants. Colorful tropical fish, sea turtles, occasionally small sharks. The coral reef has some degradation but is still interesting.

Best spots: Near Isla Grande (La Piscinita natural pool), the sunken airplane wreck, and quieter islands away from boat traffic.

April conditions: Dry season = calm seas and good visibility. April is one of the best months for water clarity.

Gear: Most tours include basic snorkel gear. Quality varies. Bring your own mask if you have one.

Snorkeling is better value than scuba here. Scuba reviews are mixed –the coral reef condition disappoints experienced divers. Stick to snorkeling as part of the day rather than booking a dedicated dive.

pool Oceanario (Isla San Martin de Pajarales)

What it is: A small open-air aquarium with saltwater pools housing dolphins, sharks, fish, and other sea life.

Reviews: Deeply mixed. Some enjoy it as a quick stop. Many travelers raise animal welfare concerns –dolphins in small basins, overall conditions questionable.

Recommendation: Skip it. Use the time for actual snorkeling or relaxing at a beach club instead. The ethical concerns are real, and the experience does not justify the detour.

06 / Cholon

The Party Island: What to Actually Expect

Cholon is a tiny beach area where boats from Cartagena congregate for day-long celebrations. It is Colombia's most famous boat party spot.

What It Is Like

Multiple boats cluster around palm-thatched structures jutting from shallow water. Reggaeton and hip hop blast from boats simultaneously. People dance on boats, swim between them with drinks, and buy food from vendors who paddle up in canoes.

The reality: it has "a bit of a Spring Break vibe" but Instagram videos exaggerate the wildness. Most visitors are not at MTV Spring Break levels. It is a lively, fun atmosphere where you swim, tan, drink Coco Locos, and meet other travelers. Think of it as a floating day party, not a nightclub.

Friday 10 Apr vibe: Fridays are quieter than weekends but still lively. Live DJs are typically only on Saturdays and Sundays, so Friday will have boat-mounted sound systems playing reggaeton/hip-hop but without the dedicated DJ acts. Less crowded = more enjoyable for a first visit. Expect 50–70% of weekend crowd levels.

Best strategy: Arrive mid-morning, spend 1–2 hours soaking up the atmosphere, then move to a quieter island for actual relaxation. Cholon as a brief stop on a private charter is ideal. An entire day dedicated to Cholon can be draining.

Cholon Survival Guide

Money: Bring 100,000–200,000 COP in cash (£20–40). No ATMs. Prices are inflated. Confirm all prices MULTIPLE TIMES before purchasing. Watch for hidden service charges.

Vendors: Be prepared to say "no, gracias" constantly. Avoid "free samples" (oysters, massages) –they always come with a bill. Only buy from your tour's recommended vendors.

Safety: Pace your drinking (you start early). Eat lunch. Drink water. Reapply sunscreen. Stay within the main boat circle (jet ski traffic outside). Watch your belongings –petty theft happens from unattended bags.

Jet skis: If you rent one, agree on EXACT time and inspect for existing damage before. Price disputes are common.

Lime warning: Fresh lime juice + sun = phytophotodermatitis (chemical burns on skin). Be careful with limes in drinks while in the sun. Rinse hands after handling.

07 / Practical Tips

Everything You Need to Know Before You Go

payments

Money & Fees

National park fee + port tax: ~34,000 COP (~£7) per person as of Aug 2025 (combined fee including mandatory medical insurance). Some sources cite a separate port tax of ~31,500 COP on top – varies by operator. Budget 40,000–65,000 COP (£8–13) per person total for dock fees. Paid at La Bodeguita dock before boarding. Some tours include this; most do not.

Cash on islands: Bring 150,000–300,000 COP (£28–60) in small bills (20,000 and 50,000 notes). No ATMs anywhere on the islands. Credit cards rarely accepted outside beach clubs.

Tipping: Tip boat crew and guides 10–15%. Cash only. 10,000–20,000 COP per person is standard.

wb_sunny

Reef-Safe Sunscreen

The Rosario Islands are a protected national marine park. Reef-safe sunscreen is strongly recommended (and increasingly enforced by tour operators).

What to look for: Free of oxybenzone and octinoxate. These chemicals bleach and kill coral reefs.

Reapply: Every 90–120 minutes, and after every swim.

Buy in Cartagena: Full bottles at city pharmacies cost £6–10. On the islands, the same bottle is £12–20.

signal_cellular_alt

Phone Signal

Coverage: Minimal to none on the islands. Expect to be essentially offline for the day. Some beach clubs have patchy WiFi but do not rely on it.

WhatsApp: The primary communication tool in Colombia. Download it before you leave home. Pre-arrange any evening plans (restaurant bookings, meeting points) before heading to the dock in the morning.

Protection: Invest in a waterproof phone case. Available for £4–12 from convenience stores in Cartagena.

book_online

How to Book

Do NOT book at the dock from street vendors. Cheap tours pack people in like sardines and upsell constantly.

Pre-book online: Tours sell out, especially beach club day passes and private charters. Book at least 1–2 weeks ahead for April (peak season).

thermostat

April Weather

Season: Tail end of dry season. One of the best months to visit. Calm seas, good visibility for snorkeling, minimal rain.

Temperature: 30–33°C (86–91°F) air, ~28°C (82°F) water.

Note: April 10 is 5 days after Easter (Easter Sunday is April 5, 2026). Semana Santa crowds may still linger. Book early.
