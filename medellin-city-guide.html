Medellín:
City of Eternal Spring

A place that was once the most dangerous city on Earth is now a genuine contender for the most exciting. The weather is 24°C every single day, the nightlife is world-class, and there’s a rawness to the culture that Lima and Bogotá have polished away.

01 / At a Glance

El Poblado Is Your Base. Laureles Is the Local Side.

El Poblado is the leafy, hilly neighbourhood where most travellers stay, packed with rooftop bars, brunch spots, and Provenza’s cafe-lined boulevard. Two full days here. Day 1 is history and local vibes, ending at a World’s 50 Best bar across the river. Day 2 is adrenaline, street art, and a cloud-forest hike. Both days end at bars ranked in the World’s 50 Best.

02 / Day 1 - Sun 5 Apr

Arrival. Light Dinner. Early Night.

Relaxed. You land at 19:35 after a full day of flying from Madrid via Bogotá. The drive from the airport winds through mountains and tunnels, and the first glimpse of Medellín’s valley of lights is genuinely spectacular.

Day 1 Timeline

José María Córdova airport. The drive to El Poblado is about 45 minutes, winding through mountains and tunnels. The first glimpse of the valley of lights is genuinely spectacular.

Drop bags, freshen up, get your bearings. You’re tired after Madrid → Bogotá → Medellín. Keep tonight simple.

Nothing fancy. Just fuel and a first taste of Medellín. Pick one:

Mondongo’s El Poblado • ~£5–8pp

Famous for bandeja paisa, the iconic Antioqueño dish: red beans, rice, chicharrón, fried egg, plantain, avocado, arepa, ground beef, chorizo. All on one plate. Absurd and essential. The right first meal in Medellín.

Bonhomia Cra. 37 #8-53 • ~£4–6pp

Italian-Colombian deli. Sharing platters of meats & cheeses, pizzas, great sangria, live music. TripAdvisor 4.8/5 (#13 in Medellín). Perfect for a tired group who just wants to graze without making decisions.

Burdo Cra. 35 #8A-101 • ~£5–8pp

Burgers, tacos, fries + cocktails. Right on the Provenza pedestrian strip. Open until 2am Fri–Sat. Your safety net if you arrive later than expected. Restaurant Guru 4.4/5 (4,100+ reviews).

03 / Day 2 - Mon 6 Apr

History, Local Vibes & World’s 50 Best

Morning is heavy: the Escobar story is real, recent, and complicated. Afternoon is the antidote – cross the river to Laureles for lunch, then pick between La 70’s bars or sunset paragliding over the valley. Either way, you end the night at Bar Carmen (#100 in the world).

Day 2 Timeline

coffee
~08:00 Breakfast on Provenza

Fuel up before the Escobar tour. All three are walkable from your accommodation and open early enough.

Pergamino Café Cra 37 #8A-37 • ~£3–5pp

Medellín’s flagship specialty coffee roaster. Granola bowls, avocado toast, baked eggs. Widely considered the best coffee in the city. TripAdvisor 4.5/5.

Ganso & Castor Cra 36 #7-46 • ~£6–8pp

Parisian-inspired café-bistro. Fluffy pancakes, eggs Benedict with chef’s special spices. “New York meets Paris” vibe. TripAdvisor Travellers’ Choice. Opens 7am.

Al Alma Provenza Cl 8 #35-37 • ~£3–5pp

Local specialty coffee chain with in-house roastery. Eggs Benedict, French toast, croissants, bagels. Indoor and outdoor seating. Budget-friendly option.

history_edu
09:00 Pablo Escobar History Tour 3–4 hrs

Guided tour of key sites from the Escobar era. The good tours are run by locals who lived through it, not glorifiers. Focus on victims and the city’s recovery, not narco-tourism glamour. Ask your hotel for a recommendation.

13:00 Laureles Afternoon

Uber across the river to Medellín’s more local, increasingly hip neighbourhood. Carrera 70 (La 70) is the main strip: a completely different energy to El Poblado. Less polished, more authentic. This is the side of Medellín most tourists don’t see.

Bárbaro Cocina Primitiva Cra 76 #73B-39 • ~£7–13pp

Fire-grilled steaks, ribs, and cuts. “Primitive cooking” concept. Generous portions. TripAdvisor 4.9/5, ranked #5 in all of Medellín. Short walk from La 70. Opens at noon.

Mondongo’s La 70 Cra 70 #Circular 3-43 • ~£6–10pp

The Laureles branch. Same iconic bandeja paisa but fewer tourists than the Poblado location. Directly on La 70. More local feel. Good for the full Antioquian spread.

Olivia Pizzeria Av 74B #39-46 • ~£5–9pp

Neapolitan wood-fired pizza and fresh pasta. Good cocktails and sangria. Lighter option if you don’t want heavy Colombian food. Near La 70. TripAdvisor 3.9/5, 223 reviews.

04 / Day 3 - Tue 7 Apr

Adrenaline, Street Art & Cloud Forest

Morning on quad bikes in the hills, then the day’s highlight: Comuna 13’s street art, escalators, and hip-hop. Ride the MetroCable to Parque Arví’s cloud forest for golden hour (Line L open Tue–Sun). Back to Provenza for dinner and rooftop cocktails.

Day 3 Timeline

coffee
~09:00 Breakfast

Fuel up before the ATVs at 10. All on or near the Provenza strip.

Pergamino Café Cra 37 #8A-37 • ~£3–5pp

Medellín’s flagship specialty coffee. Waffles, baked eggs, pastries. Opens 7am. TripAdvisor 4.5/5.

Al Alma Provenza Cl 8 #35-37 • ~£4–7pp

In-house roastery. Eggs Benedict, French toast, croissants, granola bowls. Colourful tiled atrium. Gets busy after 11am. Opens 8am.

Hija Mia Coffee Roasters Cl 11A #43B-9, Manila • ~£4–6pp

Australian-owned third-wave roasters. Avocado smash on sourdough, acai bowls, pancakes. Small-batch Colombian beans roasted on-site. TripAdvisor 4.7/5 (#5 Coffee & Tea in Medellín).

two_wheeler
10:00 ATV / Quad Bike Trails 2–3 hrs

Medellín’s surrounding hills are laced with off-road trails. Mud routes, river crossings, and jungle-edge paths with valley views. Most tours depart from the south of the city and include transport to the trail start point.

terrain Mud, hills, river crossings, coffee farm stops
shield Safety briefing included, no experience needed
checkroom Wear clothes you don’t mind getting dirty. Trainers, not sandals.

If ATVs aren’t your thing:

Half-Day Coffee Farm Tour ~£43pp • 4 hrs

Tranvia + Metro Cable to a family coffee farm in La Sierra. Hands-on: planting, roasting, tasting. The cable car ride alone is a highlight with panoramic views. Book at medellin-tours.com.

Paintball ~£36pp • 3–4 hrs inc. transport

Outdoor scenarios on the outskirts of the city. Pickup from El Poblado, 30-min drive to the camp. Full gear, marker, and 100 paintballs included. Good for a stag. Book via GetYourGuide or Viator.

~17:00 Parque Arví via MetroCable Line L – Open Tue–Sun

From Comuna 13, Uber to Acevedo metro station and take MetroCable Line L up into the cloud forest. 16,000 hectares of Andean forest above the city with 54km of trails, butterfly gardens, and the Mercado Arví artisan market. The cable car ride itself is a highlight – 20 minutes floating over green hillsides.

06 / Where to Eat

From Street Empanadas to Michelin Stars

Curated from 50 Best Latin America, OAD, Lonely Planet, Tulio Recomienda, and Time Out. Colombia grows some of the world’s best coffee and the street food alone is worth the flight.

star

Elcielo

 Michelin-starred 50 Best LATAM 4.3/5

Colombia’s first Michelin-starred chef, Juan Manuel Barrientos. 18–19 course theatrical tasting menu over 2–3 hours, including the signature “choco-therapy” where your hands are plunged into warm chocolate before the meal begins. Draws from every Colombian region: Caribbean coast, Amazon, Andes. Five lads who are into food will talk about it for years.
