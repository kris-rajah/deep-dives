Medellín:
City of Eternal Spring

A place that was once the most dangerous city on Earth is now a genuine contender for the most exciting. The weather is 24°C every single day, the nightlife is world-class, and there’s a rawness to the culture that Lima and Bogotá have polished away.

01 / At a Glance

El Poblado Is Your Base. Laureles Is the Local Side.

El Poblado is the leafy, hilly neighbourhood where most travellers stay, packed with rooftop bars, brunch spots, and Provenza’s cafe-lined boulevard. Two full days here. Day 1 is history and street art, ending with rooftop cocktails. Day 2 is adrenaline and local vibes. Both days end at bars ranked in the World’s 50 Best.

02 / Day 1 — Sun 5 Apr

Arrival. Light Dinner. Early Night.

Relaxed. You land at 19:35 after a full day of flying from Madrid via Bogotá. The drive from the airport winds through mountains and tunnels, and the first glimpse of Medellín’s valley of lights is genuinely spectacular.

Day 1 Timeline

flight_land
19:35 Land at MDE

José María Córdova airport. The drive to El Poblado is about 45 minutes, winding through mountains and tunnels. The first glimpse of the valley of lights is genuinely spectacular.

hotel
~20:30 Check In, El Poblado

Drop bags, freshen up, get your bearings. You’re tired after Madrid → Bogotá → Medellín. Keep tonight simple.

restaurant
~21:00 Light Dinner

Nothing fancy. Just fuel and a first taste of Medellín. Pick one:

Mondongo’s El Poblado • ~£5–8pp

Famous for bandeja paisa, the iconic Antioqueño dish: red beans, rice, chicharrón, fried egg, plantain, avocado, arepa, ground beef, chorizo. All on one plate. Absurd and essential. The right first meal in Medellín.

Bonhomia Cra. 37 #8-53 • ~£4–6pp

Italian-Colombian deli. Sharing platters of meats & cheeses, pizzas, great sangria, live music. TripAdvisor 4.8/5 (#13 in Medellín). Perfect for a tired group who just wants to graze without making decisions.

Burdo Cra. 35 #8A-101 • ~£5–8pp

Burgers, tacos, fries + cocktails. Right on the Provenza pedestrian strip. Open until 2am Fri–Sat. Your safety net if you arrive later than expected. Restaurant Guru 4.4/5 (4,100+ reviews).

bedtime
~23:00 Early Night

Tomorrow starts with history and ends on a rooftop ranked #81 in the world. Get some sleep.

03 / Day 2 — Mon 6 Apr

History, Street Art & Rooftop Cocktails

Packed. The most culturally dense day of the trip. Morning is heavy: the Escobar story is real, recent, and complicated. Afternoon is the antidote: colour, music, and a community that rebuilt itself. Evening ends at a World’s 50 Best rooftop.

Day 2 Timeline

coffee
~08:00 Breakfast on Provenza

Fuel up before the Escobar tour. All three are walkable from your accommodation and open early enough.

Pergamino Café Cra 37 #8A-37 • ~£3–5pp

Medellín’s flagship specialty coffee roaster. Granola bowls, avocado toast, baked eggs. Widely considered the best coffee in the city. TripAdvisor 4.5/5.

Ganso & Castor Cra 36 #7-46 • ~£6–8pp

Parisian-inspired café-bistro. Fluffy pancakes, eggs Benedict with chef’s special spices. “New York meets Paris” vibe. TripAdvisor Travellers’ Choice. Opens 7am.

Al Alma Provenza Cl 8 #35-37 • ~£3–5pp

Local specialty coffee chain with in-house roastery. Eggs Benedict, French toast, croissants, bagels. Indoor and outdoor seating. Budget-friendly option.

history_edu
09:00 Pablo Escobar History Tour 3–4 hrs

Guided tour of key sites from the Escobar era. The good tours are run by locals who lived through it, not glorifiers. Focus on victims and the city’s recovery, not narco-tourism glamour. Ask your hotel for a recommendation.

museum Casa Museo Pablo Escobar
park Parque Inflexión (former Monaco building, now memorial park)
directions_car Transport between sites included
lunch_dining
~13:00 Lunch Between Tours

Escobar tour finishes around 12:30. Quick refuel before heading to Comuna 13 at 14:00. Either grab something near downtown or head back to El Poblado.

Hatoviejo Centro or CC Oviedo • ~£7–10pp

Iconic Antioquian chain, 40+ years old. The go-to for an authentic bandeja paisa, ajiaco, sancocho. If the tour ends near downtown, the Centro branch is right there. Also one at CC Oviedo in El Poblado.

Alambique Cl 10 #43A-30, Parque El Poblado • ~£10–13pp

Speakeasy-style entrance through a shop into a lush courtyard. Contemporary Colombian fusion, inventive dishes. Quirky, vibrant decor. TripAdvisor 4.5/5, 625 reviews.

OCI.mde Cra 33 #7-21, Provenza • ~£8–12pp

Sharing-plate concept. Pacific-caught fish, Colombian/Asian spices, organic herbs. Pleasant terrace. Walking distance to Comuna 13 pickup. TripAdvisor 4.5/5, 850+ reviews.

palette
14:00 Comuna 13 Graffiti Tour 2.5–3 hrs

THE highlight. Once the most dangerous neighbourhood in Medellín, now transformed by outdoor escalators, world-class street art, and a community that tells its story through art, music, and hip-hop. A guided walk is essential: the murals only make sense with context.

escalator Escalators: 6 sections built into the hillside. Ride to the top for panoramic views.
brush Street art: Every wall tells a story. Guides explain the symbolism.
music_note Live hip-hop & breakdancing: Street performers at various stops. Tips appreciated.
local_cafe Coffee stops: Small cafés on the route. Excellent local beans.
landscape Viewpoints: Multiple mirador spots. Late afternoon light is best.

Comfortable shoes (hilly, uneven). Bring cash for tips, street food, local art.

wb_twilight
~17:00 Golden Hour. Pick One. Optional

Comuna 13 finishes around 16:30. You have a couple of hours before dinner. Sunset drinks, a viewpoint, or just head back and rest.

Envy Rooftop (The Charlee Hotel) Cl 9A #37-16 • ~£9–11/cocktail

Medellín’s most iconic rooftop. 17th floor, 180-degree city views, pool, live DJs. Perfect for golden hour. Smart casual. TripAdvisor 4.6/5.

Zarzo Bar (Hotel El Zarzo) Cra 38 #10A-29 • ~£7–10/cocktail

6th-floor rooftop, boutique hotel. Signature cocktails with Colombian ingredients, live jazz/soul sessions. More intimate and less touristy than Envy. Panoramic views over Provenza.

Cerro Nutibara / Pueblito Paisa Free entry • 15-min taxi (~£3)

Small hill topped with a replica traditional Antioquian village. The real draw is the panoramic observation deck overlooking the entire valley. Excellent at sunset. Open daily until 10pm. Lonely Planet recommended.

restaurant
19:00 Dinner

All three are on or near the Provenza strip. Pick one:

Sambombi Bistró Local 50 Best LATAM #98 • ~£20–35pp

Menu changes weekly based on what local farmers bring in. Cassava gnocchi, tuna tostadas, roast chicken with shiitake. Wooden tables, no dress code, generous portions. The best-value serious meal in Medellín.

Cra. 35 #7-10, Provenza • Book via WhatsApp: +57 305 487 5058

La Causa Poblado Cra 33 #8A-41 • ~£12–18pp

Nikkei (Peruvian-Japanese fusion). Standout ceviches, sushi rolls, tiraditos, lomo saltado. Modern atmosphere, great for sharing. TripAdvisor 4.5/5, ranked #41 in Medellín.

OCI.mde Cra 33 #7-21 • ~£12–20pp

Sharing-plate concept. Pacific-caught fish, Colombian/Asian spices, organic herbs. Communal eating style, ideal for a group of 5. Pleasant terrace. TripAdvisor 4.5/5, 850+ reviews.

local_bar
21:00 Late Night Drinks

All walkable from Provenza. Mamba Negra is the headline act but the other two are excellent if you want a different vibe or can’t get a good spot.

Mamba Negra #81 World’s 50 Best Bars • ~£10/cocktail

Rooftop in El Poblado. Cocktail menu built around Colombian ingredients: cacao, tropical fruits, local spirits. No reservations needed but arrive by 8–9pm for a good spot.

Cl. 2 #20-50, El Poblado • Mon–Sat

El Botánico Cra 33 #7-41 • ~£8–11/cocktail

Cocktail bar built around a “travelling botanist” concept. Chef-developed cocktails using exotic herbs and spices. Lush, plant-filled interior. TripAdvisor 4.4/5. More creative and upscale than typical Provenza nightlife.

Zarzo Bar Cra 38 #10A-29 • ~£7–10/cocktail

6th-floor rooftop at boutique Hotel El Zarzo. Signature Colombian cocktails, live jazz/soul/funk sessions. Intimate, less touristy. Good for conversation. Open until 2am.

04 / Day 3 — Tue 7 Apr

Adrenaline, Local Vibes & World’s 50 Best

Packed. Morning on quad bikes in the hills, then Uber across the river to Laureles for lunch, exploring, and drinks on La 70. Optional paragliding at sunset. Nightcap at Bar Carmen, #100 in the world.

Day 3 Timeline

coffee
~09:00 Breakfast

Fuel up before the ATVs at 10. All on or near the Provenza strip.

Pergamino Café Cra 37 #8A-37 • ~£3–5pp

Medellín’s flagship specialty coffee. Waffles, baked eggs, pastries. Opens 7am. TripAdvisor 4.5/5.

Al Alma Provenza Cl 8 #35-37 • ~£4–7pp

In-house roastery. Eggs Benedict, French toast, croissants, granola bowls. Colourful tiled atrium. Gets busy after 11am. Opens 8am.

Hija Mia Coffee Roasters Cl 11A #43B-9, Manila • ~£4–6pp

Australian-owned third-wave roasters. Avocado smash on sourdough, acai bowls, pancakes. Small-batch Colombian beans roasted on-site. TripAdvisor 4.7/5 (#5 Coffee & Tea in Medellín).

two_wheeler
10:00 ATV / Quad Bike Trails 2–3 hrs

Medellín’s surrounding hills are laced with off-road trails. Mud routes, river crossings, and jungle-edge paths with valley views. Most tours depart from the south of the city and include transport to the trail start point.

terrain Mud, hills, river crossings, coffee farm stops
shield Safety briefing included, no experience needed
checkroom Wear clothes you don’t mind getting dirty. Trainers, not sandals.

If ATVs aren’t your thing:

Half-Day Coffee Farm Tour ~£43pp • 4 hrs

Tranvia + Metro Cable to a family coffee farm in La Sierra. Hands-on: planting, roasting, tasting. The cable car ride alone is a highlight with panoramic views. Book at medellin-tours.com.

Paintball ~£36pp • 3–4 hrs inc. transport

Outdoor scenarios on the outskirts of the city. Pickup from El Poblado, 30-min drive to the camp. Full gear, marker, and 100 paintballs included. Good for a stag. Book via GetYourGuide or Viator.

explore
13:00 Laureles Afternoon

Uber across the river to Medellín’s more local, increasingly hip neighbourhood. Carrera 70 (La 70) is the main strip: a completely different energy to El Poblado. Less polished, more authentic. This is the side of Medellín most tourists don’t see.

Bárbaro Cocina Primitiva Cra 76 #73B-39 • ~£7–13pp

Fire-grilled steaks, ribs, and cuts. “Primitive cooking” concept. Generous portions. TripAdvisor 4.9/5, ranked #5 in all of Medellín. Short walk from La 70. Opens at noon.

Mondongo’s La 70 Cra 70 #Circular 3-43 • ~£6–10pp

The Laureles branch. Same iconic bandeja paisa but fewer tourists than the Poblado location. Directly on La 70. More local feel. Good for the full Antioquian spread.

Olivia Pizzeria Av 74B #39-46 • ~£5–9pp

Neapolitan wood-fired pizza and fresh pasta. Good cocktails and sangria. Lighter option if you don’t want heavy Colombian food. Near La 70. TripAdvisor 3.9/5, 223 reviews.

paragliding
~16:30 Paragliding at San Félix Optional

Tandem paragliding over Medellín’s valley. Launch from San Félix, a hillside village ~30–40 minutes south of El Poblado, and fly over the entire city. On a clear day you can see the full length of the valley below. Book a sunset slot if available: the light is extraordinary.

15–20 min Flight time
2–2.5 hrs Total with transport
£20–30pp 100–150K COP
No exp. Pilot does everything

GoPro footage usually available for purchase from your pilot.

nightlife
19:00 La 70 Drinks

Stay in Laureles for the evening. La 70 comes alive at night. Hop between these before heading back to El Poblado for Bar Carmen.

La Tienda de la 70 Cra 70 #Circular 3-28 • ~£2–5/drink

THE bar on La 70. Full-on Colombian party. “Fonda” decor with balloons, flashing lights, and a giant pig. Rounds of Aguardiente, dancing on tables, live acts. Loud, bright, quintessentially Colombian. Gets lively from 8–9pm.

La Charcu 70 Cra 70 #Circular 2-48 • ~£2–6/drink

Craft beer and cocktail bar. More laid back than La Tienda. Good selection of craft beers, well-made cocktails, sharing plates of nachos, empanadas, charcuterie. Good to start the evening before things ramp up.

Son Havana Cra 73 #44-56 • ~£2–4/drink

Iconic salsa bar with live bands. Cuban-themed, packed dance floor, open since 2010. Typically Wed–Sat only. Check ahead for Tuesday opening (+57 320 451 2267). If closed, stick to La Tienda + La Charcu.

local_bar
~22:00 Bar Carmen #100 World’s 50 Best Bars

Uber back to El Poblado for a nightcap. Intimate courtyard setting, culinary cocktails that change seasonally. Low-lit, candles, small groups. ~$11/cocktail. No reservations but it fills up. Arrive by 9–10pm.

Cra. 36 #10a-27, El Poblado

Alternative blow-out Elcielo Michelin-starred

Colombia’s first Michelin-starred chef, Juan Manuel Barrientos. 18–19 course theatrical tasting menu over 2–3 hours, including the signature “choco-therapy” where your hands are plunged into warm chocolate before the meal begins. Draws from every Colombian region: Caribbean coast, Amazon, Andes.

~£140pp • ~£225pp with wine pairing • £89pp deposit at booking

Calle 7D #43C-36, El Poblado • elcielo.com.co/medellin
