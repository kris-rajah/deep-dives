01 / What Is ASML

The Company Behind Every Advanced Chip on Earth

ASML Holdings N.V., headquartered in Veldhoven, Netherlands, manufactures the photolithography systems that print nanoscale circuit patterns onto silicon wafers. Founded in 1984 as a joint venture between ASM International and Philips, it has grown into a €570 billion market cap global monopoly. Every advanced processor from Apple, Nvidia, AMD, and Qualcomm requires ASML’s machines.

How Lithography Works

Semiconductor lithography is conceptually simple: project a pattern of light through a mask onto a photosensitive silicon wafer, then chemically etch the exposed areas to create transistor circuits. In practice, it requires extraordinary engineering precision. Modern EUV systems use 13.5nm wavelength light (vs 193nm in older DUV) to print features smaller than individual virus particles.

02 / The Monopoly Thesis

Barriers Measured in Decades and Tens of Billions

ASML’s competitive position extends beyond market share to structural barriers that render displacement functionally impossible over investment-relevant time horizons. This isn’t a competitive advantage — it’s an unbreachable moat.

science

Capital Barrier

ASML invested €10B+ and 20+ years to bring EUV to viability. Replication requires $25B+ over 30+ years — no competitor has demonstrated willingness to undertake this.

lightbulb

Knowledge Barrier

15,000+ patents and a collaborative ecosystem with Zeiss (optics), TRUMPF (lasers), and dozens of university and government research partners worldwide.

hub

Supply Chain Barrier

5,100+ suppliers. Carl Zeiss SMT is sole EUV optics provider (ASML owns 24.9%). TRUMPF is sole high-power CO2 laser provider. ASML would “effectively cease to conduct business” without Zeiss.

handshake

Customer Switching Costs

Chipmakers invest 18–24 months integrating each system. ASML dedicates teams of 1,500 employees to single customers. 20–30 year relationships with every major chipmaker.

update

Installed Base Revenue

€8.2B in recurring service revenue with 20+ year service life per system. At ~60% gross margins, this alone would be a highly valuable standalone business.

rocket_launch

Widening Moat

High-NA EUV at €360–400M each raises the bar further. Each technology generation makes the moat wider, not narrower.

The Hidden Gem: Installed Base Management

IBM revenue reached €8.2B in 2025 — comparable to ASML’s total company revenue in 2017. Operating at ~60% gross margins with decade-long customer contracts, now generating ~30% of total gross profit. At current run rates (~€2.1–2.4B/quarter), IBM alone would generate ~€9B annually, providing a substantial floor during any downturn. This is transforming ASML from a pure-play capex cyclical into a company with significant recurring revenue characteristics.

The 2022 Lesson: -50% Peak-to-Trough

ASML fell from $833 (2021 peak) to $415 (2022 trough) — a -50.1% drawdown. Yet underlying financials held: FY2022 revenue still grew 13.8% YoY, and FCF was €7.2B. The drawdown was driven by multiple compression (49x → 30x P/E) during the rate hiking cycle, not fundamental deterioration. Anyone who bought the trough saw +160% returns within 18 months.

Overcapacity Risk: Present but Buffered

Chipmakers are “showing restraint amid the AI boom” — seasoned by previous boom-bust cycles. TSMC’s 2026 capex contributes “little to 2026 output and only marginally to 2027” due to multi-year lags. 06 / The China Question

Real Progress. Overstated Near-Term Risk.

The bear case centres on China. After extensive research into GDP-energy decoupling, hidden lithography advancement, and EUV prototypes, the conclusion is nuanced: progress is real and accelerating, but the gap between prototype and volume manufacturing remains years, not quarters.

07 / Risk Assessment & Valuation

At ~50x Trailing, Minor Disappointments Trigger Outsized Drawdowns

public

Taiwan Strait Risk

TSMC is ~25–30% of ASML revenue. Taiwan produces ~90% of advanced chips. ASML and TSMC can remotely disable EUV machines if China invades. TSMC diversifying ($165B Arizona, Japan, Europe) but this takes years.

groups

Customer Concentration

TSMC, Samsung, and Intel represent ~60% of revenue. Intrinsic to the industry — only a handful of companies can operate leading-edge fabs. Mitigated by switching impossibility and 20–30 year relationships.

sync

Cyclicality

Semi equipment is one of the most cyclical industries (40–50% declines in downturns). ASML’s leading-edge focus and €8.2B installed base provide a meaningful floor, but system revenue remains deeply cyclical.

precision_manufacturing

Alternative Patterning (NIL)

Canon’s nanoimprint lithography is cheaper and more energy-efficient but faces fundamental limitations: catastrophic yield risk, particle contamination, template wear. “Partial replacement on selected layers” not a substitute for EUV on critical layers.

Standalone Assessment: When ASML Is Worth Owning

What It Does Well

  • • Unbreakable monopoly widening with each generation
  • • €8B+ recurring revenue flywheel at 60% margins
  • • Sits at intersection of every secular tech trend
  • • Conservative balance sheet (D/E 0.14x)

What It Doesn’t

  • • 3 customers = ~60% of revenue
  • • Caught between US, Dutch, Chinese, Taiwanese interests
  • • New system revenue deeply cyclical
  • • At 50x trailing, minor misses trigger 30–40% drops

The closest analogy is a toll road for the digital economy — everyone who wants to build advanced chips must pay ASML’s toll.

Verdict

Conviction Intact. Monitor Quarterly. Size Conservatively.

ASML’s monopoly is time-limited rather than permanent, but the timeline extends beyond most investment horizons. At ~€1,365, it is fairly valued on a base case and somewhat expensive on a bear case — not a screaming buy, but the monopoly economics, structural demand tailwinds, and growing recurring revenue base make it one of the highest-quality compounders in global equities. The optimal strategy is to accumulate during the inevitable 30–40% corrections rather than chasing all-time highs.
