04 / Risk Assessment

Valuation Premium. Key-Man Risk. Currency Headwinds.

India is not without risk. The market trades at a significant premium to emerging market peers, governance relies on a single fund manager, and currency depreciation is structural. The honest assessment: strong structural tailwinds offset by real near-term vulnerabilities.

trending_up

Bull Arguments

1. Structural Growth Is Multi-Decade

India’s demographic dividend, digital infrastructure, and urbanisation wave are 30+ year trends. Even with near-term volatility, the direction of travel is unambiguous.

2. Jupiter’s GARP Discipline Protects Downside

At 16.7x P/E vs the 20x benchmark, the fund avoids the worst of India’s valuation premium. Max drawdown of -38.2% vs -42.1% for MSCI India during COVID — demonstrating downside resilience.

3. Monetary Policy Tailwind

RBI’s 125bps of rate cuts in 2025 provide a meaningful boost to credit growth and equity valuations. India is cutting while the West holds — a rare divergence favouring EM assets.

trending_down

Bear Arguments

1. India’s Valuation Premium Is Stretched

MSCI India at 20x P/E represents a 64% premium to the EM average of 12.2x. If global risk appetite fades, the premium compresses first — India falls further than peers.

2. Key-Man Risk Is Real

Vazirani is the sole decision-maker. Morningstar’s “Average” People Pillar rating reflects this concentration. 18 years of alpha could evaporate with a single departure.

3. China Competition Looms Large

China controls 45% of global manufacturing vs India’s 3.3%. “Make in India” has underdelivered at 15.9% of GDP vs the 25% target. The China+1 thesis is selective, not structural.

Valuation Premium 20x P/E

64% premium to EM (12.2x). Jupiter mitigates at 16.7x, but a rising-rate or risk-off environment would compress multiples across the board.

Key-Man Risk 1 Manager

Vazirani is sole decision-maker. Morningstar “Average” People Pillar. No clear succession plan publicly disclosed.

Coalition Government BJP -40

BJP lost outright majority in June 2024, now governing via coalition. Reform pace may slow. Key risk: dilution of GST reforms and infrastructure pipeline.

Currency Exposure 1.5–2%

Annual GBP drag from INR depreciation. Structural and persistent — not hedgeable at reasonable cost for retail investors. Accepted as cost of India exposure.

China Competition 45% vs 3.3%

China’s global manufacturing share dwarfs India’s. China+1 benefits are real but limited to specific sectors (electronics, pharma, auto components).

Mitigating Factor 16.7x

Jupiter’s portfolio P/E trades at a meaningful discount to the 20x benchmark. GARP discipline provides a partial buffer against India-wide valuation risk.
